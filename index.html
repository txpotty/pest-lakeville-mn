Common Pest Problems and Solutions

Understanding the types of pests you might encounter and the issues they cause is the first step in effectively tackling an infestation. In Lakeville, MN, we deal with a variety of common household and commercial pests. Recognizing the signs of these invaders early can save you time, money, and health problems. Here’s a look at some prevalent pest issues our community faces and how we provide solutions.

Ant Infestations

Ants are a ubiquitous problem, seemingly appearing out of nowhere in kitchens, bathrooms, and other areas where food or moisture are present. While many ant species are relatively harmless, some, like carpenter ants, can cause structural damage by tunneling through wood. Others, like odorous house ants, can contaminate food with their trails. A visible line of ants marching across your counter is a clear sign of a problem. Beyond visible trails, you might find small piles of sawdust (frass) from carpenter ants or discover ants around pet food dishes or overflowing garbage cans. Our ant control service involves identifying the species of ant, locating their nests, and applying targeted treatments to eliminate the colony. We also focus on preventative measures, such as sealing entry points and advising on proper food storage, to stop future invasions. Understanding ant behavior, like trailing patterns and food preferences, allows us to use baits and barriers that are most effective for the specific ant problem you’re facing. We don’t just spray; we strategize to eradicate the source of the ant problem.

Rodent Problems

Rodents, such as mice and rats, are not only unsettling to find in your property but also pose serious health risks and can cause significant damage. They chew through electrical wires, insulation, wood, and even pipes, potentially leading to fires, water damage, and structural compromise. Their droppings and urine can contaminate food surfaces and air, spreading diseases like hantavirus and salmonella. Signs of a rodent infestation include finding droppings, gnaw marks on surfaces or food packaging, scurrying sounds in walls or attics, and discovering nests made of shredded materials. You might also notice a musky odor. Our rodent control strategy includes trapping, baiting, and exclusion. We identify how rodents are entering your property and seal those entry points to prevent re-infestation. We also educate you on ways to make your home less attractive to rodents, such as storing food in airtight containers and keeping outdoor areas tidy. Our methods are humane and effective, ensuring your property is free from these unwelcome guests and the potential health and safety hazards they bring. Rodent control is about more than just removing the visible pests; it’s about securing your property against future intrusions.

Bed Bug Infestations

Bed bugs are notoriously difficult to get rid of without professional help. These tiny, elusive pests feed on blood, typically at night while you sleep. They cause itchy bites and significant anxiety. Signs of bed bugs include finding small, reddish-brown stains (fecal spots) on bedding or mattresses, discovering shed skins or eggshells in crevices, or noticing a sweet, musty odor in infested areas. Bed bugs can hide in mattresses, box springs, furniture, cracks in walls, and even electrical outlets, making them hard to find and eradicate. Our bed bug service employs a multi-faceted approach that may include thorough inspections, heat treatments, conventional chemical treatments, and follow-up visits to ensure complete eradication. Heat treatments are particularly effective as they penetrate all cracks and crevices where bed bugs hide and kill all life stages, including eggs. We also advise on proper preparation for treatment, such as washing and drying all infested linens and clothing on high heat, to maximize the effectiveness of our service. Dealing with bed bugs requires patience and expertise, and we have both.

Termite Damage

Termites are silent destroyers that can cause extensive and costly structural damage to your home before you even realize you have a problem. They feed on cellulose, which is abundant in wood, and can compromise the integrity of beams, walls, and floors. Swarming termites, which are winged adults looking to establish new colonies, are often the first visible sign of an infestation. You might also find discarded wings near windowsills. Other signs include mud tubes on foundations or walls, hollow-sounding wood when tapped, or blistering paint. Termite damage often goes unnoticed within walls until it becomes severe. Our termite treatment involves a comprehensive inspection to identify the species of termite and the extent of the activity. We then apply targeted treatments, which may include liquid termiticides applied to the soil around your foundation or baiting systems that termites consume and share with the colony, leading to its elimination. Preventative treatments are also essential, especially in areas prone to termite activity. Early detection and treatment are crucial to minimizing the damage caused by these destructive pests. We provide thorough inspections and effective treatment plans to protect your investment.

Spider Worries

Spiders are a common sight in homes and businesses, especially in quiet, undisturbed areas like basements, attics, and garages. While most spiders found in Minnesota are harmless, their presence can be unsettling for many people. Some spiders can deliver painful bites, and the accumulation of webs can make a property look neglected. Frequent sightings of spiders and an increase in their webbing activity are obvious signs of a spider problem. You might also find egg sacs, which indicate reproduction. Our spider control service includes identifying the types of spiders present and implementing strategies to reduce their populations and prevent them from entering your property. This often involves physical removal of spiders and webs, applying targeted insecticides to key areas where spiders hide, and sealing entry points to deter them. We also advise on practices that make your property less appealing to spiders, such as reducing clutter and managing other insect populations, as spiders feed on insects. Our goal is to reduce spider populations and their activity to a comfortable level, ensuring your peace of mind.

Cockroach Concerns

Cockroaches are persistent and resilient pests known for their ability to spread bacteria and pathogens, contaminating food, surfaces, and utensils. They can exacerbate allergies and asthma, particularly in children. Seeing live cockroaches, especially during the day (as they are primarily nocturnal), is a strong indicator of an infestation. You might also find cockroach droppings, which look like coffee grounds or black pepper, egg cases (oothecae), or notice a foul, musty odor. Cockroaches often hide in dark, damp areas like kitchens, bathrooms, and basements. Our cockroach control involves a multi-pronged approach, including thorough cleaning recommendations to eliminate food sources and harborage areas, applying targeted insecticides and baits in areas where cockroaches hide and travel, and sealing entry points. Different cockroach species require different treatment strategies, so accurate identification is key. We work to not only eliminate the current infestation but also to prevent future ones by addressing the conditions that attract cockroaches. Getting rid of a cockroach problem often requires professional expertise due to their rapid reproduction rate and ability to develop resistance to insecticides.

Flea Infestations

Fleas are tiny, wingless insects that primarily feed on the blood of mammals and birds. While they are often associated with pets, they can also bite humans. Flea bites are typically itchy and can cause allergic reactions. Heavy infestations can lead to anemia, especially in young animals. Signs of fleas include seeing your pets scratching excessively, discovering “flea dirt” (feces that look like black pepper flakes) on your pet’s fur or bedding, or experiencing bites on your ankles or legs. Fleas can also infest carpets, furniture, and cracks in flooring. Our flea treatment involves treating both the infested animals (in conjunction with your veterinarian) and the environment where fleas are present. Environmental treatments include thorough vacuuming (and immediately discarding the vacuum bag outside) and applying insecticides to affected areas, such as carpets, upholstery, and pet resting areas. Multiple treatments are often necessary to target all life stages of the flea, including eggs, larvae, and pupae. We also provide advice on preventing future flea problems, such as using veterinarian-approved flea and tick control products on your pets and regularly cleaning pet areas.

Bee and Wasp Removal

Bees and wasps can be beneficial pollinators, but when they establish nests near homes or businesses, they can pose a stinging hazard, especially to individuals with allergies. Identifying the type of stinging insect is crucial, as treatment methods vary. Signs of a problem include seeing increased numbers of bees or wasps around your property, observing them entering or exiting a specific location (like a hole in the ground or under eaves), or discovering a visible nest. Removing a bee or wasp nest can be dangerous, and it’s best left to professionals. Our bee and wasp removal service prioritizes safety and effectiveness. We carefully assess the location and type of nest and use appropriate methods to safely remove or treat the nest. We also take precautions to minimize the risk of stings during the process. For species like honeybees, we may recommend contacting a local beekeeper for relocation if possible. For aggressive species like yellow jackets or hornets, direct treatment to eliminate the colony is often necessary. We also offer preventative measures to deter stinging insects from nesting on your property in the future.

Mosquito Control

Mosquitoes are not just annoying biters; they can also transmit diseases like West Nile virus. Their presence can significantly reduce the enjoyment of outdoor spaces during warmer months. Excessive mosquito activity around your home, noticing itchy bites after being outdoors, and the presence of standing water (breeding grounds for mosquitoes) are all signs of a potential mosquito problem. Our mosquito control service aims to reduce mosquito populations around your property, allowing you to enjoy your yard again. We conduct a thorough inspection to identify potential breeding sites, such as standing water in gutters, bird baths, or containers. Our treatment involves applying larvicides to standing water that cannot be eliminated and using adulticides to target mosquitoes resting in vegetation around your property. We also provide recommendations for eliminating standing water sources and other preventative measures you can take to reduce mosquito populations. Our goal is to create a barrier of protection around your home, significantly reducing the number of bothersome and potentially dangerous mosquitoes.

Silverfish Issues

Silverfish are nocturnal, wingless insects that prefer damp, dark environments. They are often found in bathrooms, basements, and attics. While not harmful to humans directly, they can damage paper products, books, wallpaper, stored foods, and clothing by feeding on starches and cellulose. Signs of silverfish include seeing the tear-drop shaped, silvery-gray insects, finding small holes or damage in paper or textiles, or discovering yellowish stains or scales left behind. Their presence often indicates an underlying moisture issue in your home. Our silverfish control involves identifying and addressing the sources of moisture that attract them, such as leaks or poor ventilation. We also apply targeted treatments in areas where silverfish are found, such as cracks and crevices. Reducing humidity levels through dehumidifiers or improving ventilation is a key preventative measure. We help you create an environment that is less hospitable to silverfish, eliminating existing populations and preventing their return.

Tick Management

Ticks are arachnids related to spiders and mites that can transmit diseases like Lyme disease to humans and pets. They are typically found in wooded areas, tall grass, and leaf litter. Finding ticks on yourself, your pets, or your clothing after spending time outdoors is a clear sign of tick presence in your environment. Depending on the type of tick, they can be as small as a poppy seed or larger when engorged with blood. Our tick control focuses on reducing tick populations in your yard and surrounding areas. This can involve treating vegetation, especially at the edges of your property where wooded areas or tall grass are present. We also provide recommendations for creating a “tick-safe zone” around your home, such as keeping lawns mowed, removing leaf litter, and creating a border of wood chips or gravel between your lawn and wooded areas. Educating yourself on tick prevention, such as wearing protective clothing and performing tick檢查s after being outdoors, is also important. Our services aim to make your outdoor spaces safer for you and your pets.

Moth Infestations

Moths can be more than just a nuisance fluttering around lights. Some species, like pantry moths and clothes moths, can cause significant damage to stored food products and textiles. Seeing adult moths flying around, finding webbing in stored food containers, discovering larvae or pupae, or noticing holes in clothing made of natural fibers are signs of a moth infestation. Pantry moths contaminate food, requiring disposal of infested items. Clothes moths can destroy valuable garments. Our moth control involves identifying the type of moth and locating the source of the infestation. For pantry moths, this means inspecting all food items, especially grains, cereals, and dry goods. For clothes moths, this involves inspecting closets, dressers, and stored textiles. Treatment includes thorough cleaning of infested areas, disposing of infested items, and applying targeted insecticides or pheromone traps to eliminate adult moths and larvae. We also provide advice on proper food storage and textile care to prevent future infestations. Addressing a moth problem requires diligence and a systematic approach to eliminate all life stages and prevent re-infestation.

Squirrel Removal

Squirrels, while seemingly harmless woodland creatures, can become a significant nuisance and cause considerable damage when they decide to make your attic, walls, or chimney their home. They can chew through electrical wires, insulation, and wood, posing fire hazards and causing structural damage. Their nesting materials can block vents and chimneys. Hearing scratching or scurrying sounds in your attic or walls, finding gnaw marks on the exterior of your home, or seeing squirrels entering or exiting small openings are all signs of a squirrel problem. Our squirrel removal service focuses on humane trapping and removal of squirrels. We identify how they are entering your property and use live traps to capture them. Once captured, squirrels are safely relocated to a more suitable habitat away from your home. After removal, it is crucial to seal all entry points to prevent other squirrels or wildlife from taking up residence. We also assess and repair any damage caused by the squirrels and provide recommendations for making your property less attractive to them, such as trimming tree branches away from your roofline. Wildlife removal requires expertise and adherence to local regulations to ensure the safety of both the animals and your property.

Raccoon Removal

Raccoons are intelligent and adaptable animals that are often attracted to urban and suburban areas due to the availability of food sources like garbage cans and pet food. While they might seem interesting, raccoons can pose a risk to property and health. They can damage roofs, chimneys, and decks while seeking shelter or searching for food. They can also carry diseases like rabies and roundworm. Finding overturned garbage cans, damage to your property, hearing noises in your attic or chimney, or seeing raccoons on your property during the day (which can be a sign of illness) are indicators of a raccoon issue. Our raccoon removal service employs safe and humane methods to remove raccoons from your property. We use live traps to capture raccoons and then relocate them to an appropriate area. It’s essential to identify and secure potential entry points to prevent re-entry. We also provide recommendations on securing garbage cans and removing other attractants. Dealing with raccoons requires caution and expertise, and our trained technicians are equipped to handle these situations safely and effectively. Ensuring your property is not an easy source of food or shelter is key to preventing future raccoon problems.
